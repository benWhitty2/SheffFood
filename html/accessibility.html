Accessibility

this website is accessible by design because of a large number of decisions that cater for all range and types of ability

impairmentaccommodation
colour blind a monotone colour scheme ensures that there is no combination of colours that make and article indiscernible from its background
visually impaired all images have a accurate and relevant alternate text to make them interpretable by screen readers
dyslexia this website avoids using large blocks of text opting for bullet points and trying to break the page up into multiple segments to make it easier to follow and keep track of and trying to contain information on most sub-subjects to one line (eg: opening and closing times of a vendor)
hearing impairments this website avoids using audio to communicate information to the user because of the difficulty of communicating tone in transcripts
motor impairments this website uses simple menus that require one click to visit any location on the website, involves minimal scrolling and all buttons are of a size that makes them easy to use to those who cannot precisely operate a mouse
